Rules and Guidelines of Hackathon Software Event
  1. Eligibility: Participants must meet the eligibility criteria specified by the event organizers.
  2. Team Formation: Participants may be allowed to form teams with a predetermined number of members.
  3. Original Work: All projects developed during the hackathon must be original and should not infringe on any copyright or intellectual property rights.
  4. Theme or Challenge: Hackathons often have a specific theme or challenge that participants must address in their projects.
  5. Time Limit: Hackathons are time-bound events, usually lasting from a few hours to several days.
  6. Code of Conduct: Participants must adhere to a code of conduct that promotes a respectful and inclusive environment for everyone involved.
  7. Technology Stack: Hackathons may specify a particular technology stack or programming language that participants should use for their projects.
  8. Submissions: Participants must submit their final projects within the specified deadline.
  9. Judging Criteria: Projects are evaluated based on various criteria, such as innovation, functionality, usability, technical complexity, and relevance to the theme.
  10. Intellectual Property: Some hackathons may have specific rules regarding the ownership of intellectual property developed during the event.
  11. Collaboration: While collaboration within teams is encouraged, participants should not collaborate with other teams to build their projects.
  12. Code Repository: Participants may be required to use a version control system (e.g., Git) to manage their code repositories.
  13. Presentations: Participants often need to present their projects to a panel of judges or the entire audience.
  14. Prizes: Hackathons may offer prizes or awards for winning teams or outstanding individual contributions.
  15. Non-Discrimination: The event should promote a diverse and inclusive atmosphere.
